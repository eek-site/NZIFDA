Mediation & Dispute Resolution

NZIFDA provides comprehensive dispute resolution services covering the full spectrum from initial mediation through to Disputes Tribunal representation. NZIFDA represents the complainant throughout the entire process, ensuring their interests are protected and their case is presented effectively.

Complete Dispute Resolution Service

NZIFDA offers a full-service dispute resolution pathway:

  1. Initial Mediation: Free mediation services to resolve disputes quickly and fairly
  2. Formal Mediation: Structured mediation sessions with neutral NZIFDA mediators
  3. Disputes Tribunal: If mediation fails, NZIFDA represents the complainant at the Disputes Tribunal
  4. Ongoing Support: Continued assistance throughout the entire process

Important: NZIFDA represents the complainant at all stages, including Disputes Tribunal proceedings. We advocate on your behalf to ensure your case is properly presented and your interests are protected.

What We Cover

Service Disputes

We mediate disputes related to:

  • Quality of fuel extraction and remediation services
  • Pricing and billing disputes
  • Warranty claims and coverage
  • Damage claims and liability
  • Documentation and record-keeping issues
  • Compliance and certification disputes

Disputes Tribunal Representation

When mediation cannot resolve a dispute, NZIFDA represents the complainant at the Disputes Tribunal. The Disputes Tribunal is New Zealand's low-cost alternative to court for disputes up to $30,000.

NZIFDA provides comprehensive representation including:

  • Professional advocacy: NZIFDA representatives present your case effectively at the tribunal
  • Case preparation: Complete preparation of all documentation, evidence, and submissions
  • Expert testimony: Industry expert testimony to support your case
  • Legal guidance: Advice on tribunal procedures and legal requirements
  • Evidence presentation: Professional presentation of all relevant evidence
  • Full representation: NZIFDA acts on your behalf throughout the entire tribunal process
  • Post-tribunal support: Assistance with enforcement of tribunal orders if needed

Disputes Tribunal Process:

  1. Application is filed with the Disputes Tribunal
  2. NZIFDA prepares your case and all documentation
  3. Hearing is scheduled (usually within 4-6 weeks)
  4. NZIFDA represents you at the hearing
  5. Tribunal makes a binding decision
  6. NZIFDA assists with enforcement if necessary

Key Point: NZIFDA represents the complainant throughout the Disputes Tribunal process. You don't need to hire a lawyer - we handle everything on your behalf.

The Complete Resolution Process

Step 1: Initial Mediation

The process begins with free initial mediation:

  1. Submit Request: File a complaint or mediation request through our complaint form
  2. Case Review: NZIFDA reviews the dispute and gathers information from all parties
  3. Initial Assessment: We assess the case and determine the best approach
  4. Mediation Session: A neutral NZIFDA mediator facilitates discussion between parties
  5. Resolution Attempt: Parties work together to reach a mutually acceptable solution
  6. Agreement: If successful, a written agreement is prepared and signed by all parties
  7. Compliance Monitoring: We monitor compliance with the agreement and provide ongoing support

Step 2: Disputes Tribunal (If Mediation Fails)

If mediation cannot resolve the dispute, NZIFDA represents the complainant at the Disputes Tribunal:

  1. Tribunal Application: NZIFDA files the application with the Disputes Tribunal on your behalf
  2. Case Preparation: Complete preparation of documentation, evidence, and legal submissions
  3. Hearing Preparation: NZIFDA prepares you and all evidence for the tribunal hearing
  4. Hearing Representation: NZIFDA represents you at the tribunal hearing, presenting your case
  5. Decision: Tribunal makes a binding decision (usually on the day or within 14 days)
  6. Enforcement: If the other party doesn't comply, NZIFDA assists with enforcement

No Additional Cost: For NZIFDA member insurers and certified operator customers, Disputes Tribunal representation is included in the mediation service.

Benefits of Mediation

  • Cost-effective: Much less expensive than litigation
  • Faster resolution: Disputes can often be resolved in days or weeks rather than months
  • Confidential: Mediation proceedings are private and confidential
  • Preserves relationships: Parties can maintain working relationships
  • Mutually acceptable: Solutions are agreed upon by all parties
  • Flexible: Creative solutions can be developed that courts cannot provide

When to Use Mediation

Ideal Situations

Mediation is particularly effective for:

  • Disputes where parties want to maintain a relationship
  • Cases where both parties want a quick resolution
  • Situations where cost is a concern
  • Disputes involving technical or industry-specific issues
  • Cases where creative solutions are needed
  • Situations where privacy is important
