NZIFDA Services

NZIFDA provides comprehensive certification services to ensure industry excellence in fuel contamination remediation.

NZIFDA Mark Certification

Two-Tier Certification Structure: NZIFDA operates a two-tier structure with three certification types to ensure quality service delivery and efficient job allocation. Compliant Operators (Tier 1) manage customer relationships and allocate work to Certified Workshops and Mobile Operators (Tier 2), both of which must operate under a Compliant Operator license. We assess and certify operators, workshops, and mobile operators who meet our rigorous standards and authorize them to display the NZIFDA Mark.

Compliant Operators:

  • Management layer that receives customer inquiries
  • Allocates jobs to Certified Workshops in their network
  • Manages customer relationships and service delivery
  • Ensures quality control across their network
  • Comprehensive compliance audits
  • Ongoing monitoring and renewal

Certified Workshops:

  • Must operate under a Compliant Operator license
  • Fixed facility requirement - all remediation must occur at certified fixed facilities
  • Equipment and fixed facility inspections
  • Staff training verification
  • Insurance and licensing verification
  • Ongoing monitoring and renewal

⚠️ Mobile extraction services are NON-COMPLIANT and cannot be certified. All remediation must occur at certified fixed facilities. Workshops must operate under a Compliant Operator license.

Compliance Monitoring & Enforcement Reporting

We actively monitor the industry to ensure all operators maintain compliance with NZIFDA standards and New Zealand regulations.

  • Regular compliance audits
  • Investigation of reported violations
  • NZIFDA Mark authorization and verification
  • Investigation of reported non-compliance issues
  • Reporting illegal operators to WorkSafe New Zealand, EPA, and local authorities
  • Supporting enforcement actions against non-compliant operators

Enforcement: Illegal operators face fines up to $600,000 and potential imprisonment. NZIFDA reports all violations to relevant authorities for prosecution.
