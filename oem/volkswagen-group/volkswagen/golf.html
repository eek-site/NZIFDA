⚠️ VW Golf TDI Diesel Misfuel Warning

If petrol has been added to a Golf TDI (diesel):

  • Do NOT start the engine
  • Do NOT turn ignition to RUN (priming pump activates)
  • Contact NZIFDA certified technician immediately

⚠️ Volkswagen Position - Golf

Volkswagen prescribes fuel tank draining using fuel cart and fuel tank removal as procedures for accessing Golf fuel tank contents.

VW service manuals explicitly state: "Empty the fuel tank before removing it" and "drain fuel tank using fuel cart." Fuel contamination is excluded from VW warranty.

Volkswagen ELSA Service Manual

"Fuel Tank Assembly, Removing And Installing

WARNING! Fire hazard! Do NOT have anything in the area that can ignite gasoline!

  • Disconnect Battery Ground strap
  • First ensure that ignition is switched OFF
  • Open fuel tank cover briefly and close again
  • If necessary, drain fuel tank using fuel cart
  • WARNING! Empty the fuel tank before removing it!"
— Volkswagen ELSA Service Manual

NZIFDA Recommended Methods

✓ Method A: Fuel Pump Access Panel Extraction

Applicable when:

  • Engine was NOT started on contaminated fuel
  • Vehicle has accessible fuel pump panel under rear seat
  • Quick drain required

✓ Method B: Full Tank Drop/Removal

Required when:

  • Engine was started/driven on contaminated fuel
  • Tank inspection required
  • Complete system flush needed

✗ Filler Neck Extraction

Not Approved - VW anti-siphon systems prevent this method. VW explicitly prescribes fuel cart drainage, not filler neck extraction.

Golf TDI Diesel Models

VW Golf TDI models use common rail diesel injection systems:

Engine Fuel System
1.6 TDI Common Rail, piezoelectric injectors
2.0 TDI Common Rail, piezoelectric injectors

⚠️ TDI System Sensitivity

VW TDI engines are extremely sensitive to fuel contamination:

  • Piezoelectric injectors can fail with minimal petrol contamination
  • High-pressure pump uses fuel for lubrication
  • Petrol destroys lubricity, causing metal-on-metal wear
  • If engine was run, complete fuel system replacement may exceed €10,000+

MQB Platform - Shared Procedures

The Golf shares the MQB platform with other VW Group vehicles. Similar fuel system procedures apply to:

  • Audi A3 (8V, 8Y)
  • ŠKODA Octavia
  • SEAT Leon
  • VW Tiguan

NZIFDA certified technicians trained on Golf procedures can apply similar approaches to these related models.

Golf GTI and Golf R

Performance variants (GTI, R) are petrol-only:

  • GTI: 2.0 TSI petrol
  • R: 2.0 TSI petrol (AWD)

Diesel-in-petrol contamination requires complete fuel drain but is generally less damaging than petrol-in-diesel.
