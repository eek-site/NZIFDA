Legal Framework for Repairs

Consumer Guarantees Act 1993

The Consumer Guarantees Act applies to repair services and requires that services be:

  • Performed with reasonable care and skill - Repairs must be completed to a professional standard
  • Fit for purpose - The repair must resolve the contamination issue
  • Completed within a reasonable time - Delays must be justified
  • Charged at a reasonable price - Costs must be fair and transparent

Insurer Liability: When insurers arrange repairs, they become responsible for ensuring these guarantees are met. Using non-certified operators increases the risk of guarantee breaches and potential liability.

Fair Trading Act 1986

The Fair Trading Act prohibits misleading and deceptive conduct in trade. This includes:

  • False or misleading representations about services, qualifications, or certification
  • Unfair practices such as charging for unnecessary work or parts
  • Bait advertising - advertising services at one price but charging more

Insurer Risk: If an insurer engages a repairer who engages in misleading conduct, the insurer may be held liable for the repairer's actions, particularly if the insurer failed to exercise due diligence in selecting the repairer.

Insurance Law & Duty of Care

Under New Zealand insurance law, insurers have a duty to:

  • Act in good faith towards policyholders
  • Exercise reasonable care in selecting repairers
  • Ensure repairs are properly completed to restore the policyholder's position
  • Prevent further loss by ensuring proper remediation

Due Diligence Requirement: Insurers must verify that repairers are qualified, certified, and compliant. Failure to do so may constitute a breach of the duty of care.

Cost Risks: Retail Rates & Fake Charges

üí∞ Excessive Costs from Non-Certified Operators

1. Retail Rates

Non-certified operators typically charge retail rates that are significantly higher than trade rates:

  • Retail rates can be 30-50% higher than trade rates available to insurers
  • No negotiated pricing agreements
  • No volume discounts
  • Each claim charged at maximum rate

2. Fake or Unnecessary Charges

Non-certified operators frequently charge for unnecessary work or parts:

  • Fuel Pump Replacement: Charging for fuel pump replacement when only cleaning is needed (saving $800-$1,500 per claim)
  • Injector Replacement: Charging for injector replacement when cleaning would suffice (saving $1,200-$2,500 per claim)
  • Fuel Filter Replacement: Charging premium prices for standard filters
  • Unnecessary Flushing: Charging for multiple flushes when one proper extraction is sufficient
  • Excessive Labor Hours: Inflating labor charges for simple extractions
  • Parts Markup: Charging retail markup on parts that should be at trade cost

Real Impact: A typical misfuel claim handled by a non-certified operator can cost $2,500-$4,000, while the same claim handled by an NZIFDA-certified operator typically costs $800-$1,200. Savings of $1,300-$2,800 per claim.

Membership Requirements

NZIFDA insurer membership is free, but all applicants are vetted to ensure they meet our standards:

  • Licensed Insurance Company: Must be a registered and licensed insurer in New Zealand
  • Fair Claims Handling: Must demonstrate fair and reasonable claims handling practices
  • Policyholder Protection: Must show commitment to protecting policyholder interests
  • Compliance: Must comply with all relevant insurance regulations and standards
  • Good Standing: Must be in good standing with regulatory authorities

Vetting typically takes 5-10 business days. Once approved, membership is ongoing with annual review.
