⚠️ Alliance Position

Nissan, Mitsubishi, and Renault prescribe fuel tank removal and/or fuel pump access panel removal as the procedures for accessing fuel tank contents.

Fuel contamination is classified as vehicle misuse across all Alliance brands and is excluded from warranty coverage. NZIFDA certified technicians follow manufacturer service manual procedures.

About the Alliance

The Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi Alliance is a Franco-Japanese strategic partnership that is one of the world's largest automotive groups. The alliance shares platforms, technologies, and components across brands.

Alliance Service Manual Procedures

"Fuel Tank Removal (Nissan)

  • Release fuel pressure
  • Disconnect negative battery terminal
  • Drain fuel tank using approved fuel recovery equipment
  • Raise vehicle on hoist
  • Disconnect filler hose, vent hose, fuel lines
  • Disconnect fuel pump/sender harness
  • Remove heat shields
  • Support tank, remove mounting bands
  • Lower tank"
— Nissan Service Manual (ESM)

"Fuel Tank Removal (Mitsubishi)

CAUTION: Before servicing the fuel system:

  • Relieve fuel system pressure
  • Disconnect battery ground cable
  • Work in well-ventilated area
  • Drain fuel from tank using approved equipment
  • Do not allow fuel to contact hot engine components"
— Mitsubishi Service Manual

⚠️ Diesel Utes - High Misfuel Risk

Nissan Navara and Mitsubishi Triton are among the most commonly misfuelled vehicles in New Zealand. Both use common rail diesel systems that are extremely sensitive to petrol contamination:

  • Do NOT start the engine if wrong fuel suspected
  • Do NOT turn ignition to "ON" - lift pump may prime system
  • Contact NZIFDA certified technician immediately
  • Full tank removal and system flush required
