⚠️ Stellantis Position (Mopar)

"Warranties do not cover damage caused by contaminated fuels, or the use of fuels other than those recommended in your Owners Manual"

Stellantis (via Mopar service documentation) prescribes fuel tank removal and fuel pump access panel removal as the procedures for accessing fuel tank contents. Filler neck extraction is not a manufacturer-documented procedure.

Stellantis/Mopar Service Procedure

"Fuel Tank - Removal

  • Release fuel system pressure
  • Disconnect and isolate negative battery cable
  • Drain fuel tank into approved container
  • Raise vehicle on hoist
  • Disconnect fuel lines at tank
  • Support tank and remove mounting straps
  • Lower tank carefully"
— Mopar Service Manual (Chrysler/Jeep/Dodge/Ram)

"Fuel System Draining (PSA Procedure)

Before any fuel system work:

  • Ensure adequate ventilation
  • Switch off ignition
  • Disconnect battery
  • Use approved fuel recovery equipment
  • Access fuel via pump module or tank removal"
— Peugeot/Citroën Service Manual

⚠️ European Diesel Vehicles - High Misfuel Risk

Stellantis European diesel vehicles (Peugeot, Citroën, Fiat, Alfa Romeo, Opel) commonly use high-pressure common rail fuel systems. These are particularly sensitive to petrol contamination:

  • Do NOT start the engine if wrong fuel is suspected
  • Priming pump may activate with ignition
  • Full tank removal and system flush typically required
  • Fuel injectors may require replacement if contaminated fuel circulated
