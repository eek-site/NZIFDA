NZIFDA is the authorized facilitator for fuel duty refund claims on contaminated fuel extracted during misfuelling incidents. All extracted fuel becomes eligible for duty recovery under NZTA guidelines. Service providers must be NZIFDA-accredited to process refund claims.

About the New Zealand Industrial Fuel Duty Agency

NZIFDA is an independent industry-established body founded in 2019 by major fuel retailers, service providers, and insurance companies to monitor misfuelling incidents and facilitate duty recovery on contaminated fuel. We operate under a Memorandum of Understanding with NZTA (Waka Kotahi) to process fuel duty refund claims for extracted contaminated fuel.

NZTA Fuel Duty Refund Scheme

Under the NZTA fuel duty regulations, contaminated fuel extracted during misfuelling incidents is eligible for duty refunds. NZIFDA facilitates this process as the authorized intermediary.

Refund Eligibility & Rates

Fuel Type Excise Duty Rate GST Component Total Refund/Litre Processing Time
Petrol (contaminated) $0.77 per litre $0.12 per litre $0.89 per litre 10 business days
Diesel (contaminated) $0.31 per litre $0.05 per litre $0.36 per litre 10 business days
Mixed contamination Pro-rata calculation Variable Case-by-case 15 business days

Claim Process for Service Providers

1

Extract & Document

Extract contaminated fuel, record volume, take photographic evidence, obtain customer signature.

2

Submit to NZIFDA

Lodge claim via portal within 48 hours with extraction certificate and volume documentation.

3

Verification

NZIFDA verifies claim, cross-references incident report, confirms accreditation status.

4

NZTA Processing

Approved claims forwarded to NZTA for duty refund processing via IRD systems.

Incident Reporting & Database Management

NZIFDA maintains New Zealand's comprehensive misfuelling incident database, providing crucial data to insurers, fuel retailers, and service providers. This data supports NZTA refund claims and industry best practice development.

Reporting Participants

  • Fuel Retailers: Report customer misfuelling incidents for insurance and safety records
  • Service Providers: Submit extraction reports for duty refund processing
  • Fleet Operators: Document incidents for fleet management and insurance
  • Insurance Companies: Access data for claims validation
  • Vehicle Manufacturers: Track warranty-related misfuelling cases

Submit Incident Report - Form NZIFDA-01

Incident Reference Number will be issued for tracking. For duty refund claims, ensure extraction certificate is attached.
