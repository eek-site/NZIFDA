⚠️ Volkswagen Group Position

Volkswagen Group prescribes fuel tank removal and draining via fuel cart as the procedure for accessing fuel tank contents.

VW Group service manuals explicitly warn against improper fuel system access. All warranty claims for fuel system damage due to contamination are excluded as misuse.

About Volkswagen Group

Volkswagen AG is one of the world's largest automotive manufacturers, headquartered in Wolfsburg, Germany. The group operates multiple brands across volume, premium, and luxury segments.

Volkswagen Group Service Manual Procedure

"Fuel Tank Assembly, Removing And Installing

WARNING! Fire hazard! Do NOT have anything in the area that can ignite gasoline!

  • Disconnect Battery Ground strap
  • First ensure that ignition is switched OFF
  • Open fuel tank cover briefly and close again
  • If necessary, drain fuel tank using fuel cart
  • WARNING! Empty the fuel tank before removing it!"
— Volkswagen/Audi ELSA Service Manual

⚠️ VW Group Anti-Siphon Statement

Volkswagen Group vehicles are equipped with sophisticated anti-siphon systems. The service manual explicitly prescribes using a fuel cart for draining, accessed via tank removal or fuel pump module. Filler neck extraction is not a documented procedure.

⚠️ European Diesel Vehicles - High Misfuel Risk

Volkswagen Group diesel vehicles are among the most commonly misfuelled in New Zealand. TDI engines are particularly sensitive to petrol contamination due to high-pressure common rail fuel systems. If petrol has been added to a TDI vehicle:

  • Do NOT start the engine
  • Do NOT turn the ignition on (priming pump activates)
  • Contact NZIFDA certified technician immediately
  • Full tank removal and system flush typically required
