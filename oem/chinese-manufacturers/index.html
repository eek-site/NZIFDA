⚠️ Chinese Manufacturer Position

Chinese manufacturers prescribe fuel tank removal and/or fuel pump access as the procedures for accessing fuel tank contents.

These manufacturers follow international standards for fuel system design and service procedures. NZIFDA certified technicians adapt standard OEM procedures to these vehicles.

✓ Many Chinese Vehicles are Electric

A significant proportion of Chinese-manufactured vehicles in NZ are battery electric (BYD Atto 3, Dolphin, Seal, MG4, MG ZS EV). Pure electric vehicles cannot be misfuelled as they have no fuel tank.

Petrol and Diesel Models

Chinese-manufactured petrol and diesel vehicles that can be subject to fuel contamination include:

Petrol Models

  • MG ZS (petrol variant)
  • MG HS (petrol variant)
  • MG3
  • Haval H6 (petrol variant)
  • Haval Jolion (petrol variant)

Diesel Models

  • GWM Cannon (ute) - Common rail diesel

Plug-in Hybrid Models

PHEV models have petrol tanks and can be subject to diesel-in-petrol contamination:

  • MG HS PHEV
  • BYD Sealion 6 (if petrol variant offered)

⚠️ GWM Cannon Diesel

The GWM Cannon is a diesel ute that can be misfuelled. Like other modern diesel vehicles:

  • Do NOT start the engine if wrong fuel suspected
  • Common rail diesel system is sensitive to petrol
  • Full tank drop typically required
  • Contact NZIFDA certified technician for service

Service Considerations

Chinese-manufactured vehicles generally follow international standards for fuel system design. NZIFDA certified technicians apply standard OEM procedures adapted for these vehicles:

  • Fuel pump access: Many models have access panels under rear seat
  • Anti-siphon devices: Standard on modern Chinese vehicles
  • Service documentation: Available through manufacturer networks
  • Parts availability: Generally good through official dealer networks
