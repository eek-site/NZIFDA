Mediation & Dispute Resolution

NZIFDA provides comprehensive dispute resolution services, from initial mediation through to Disputes Tribunal representation. Our expert mediators help resolve disputes between consumers, insurers, fuel stations, and operators fairly and efficiently.

Free Mediation for Members

Mediation services are free for:

  • NZIFDA member insurers and their policyholders
  • Consumers who used NZIFDA-certified operators
  • Fuel stations working with certified operators

Our Mediation Process

1Initial Complaint & Assessment

When a dispute arises, parties can file a complaint through our complaint form. Our team will:

  • Review the complaint and gather relevant documentation
  • Assess whether the matter is suitable for mediation
  • Contact all parties to understand their positions
  • Determine if the dispute involves a certified operator or member insurer

Timeline: Initial assessment typically completed within 5 business days.

2Mediation Session

If mediation is appropriate, we arrange a mediation session with an independent, qualified mediator:

  • Neutral Mediator: Experienced in fuel contamination remediation and insurance disputes
  • Expert Assessment: Technical evaluation of repair quality, compliance, and costs
  • Fair Process: All parties have equal opportunity to present their case
  • Confidential: Mediation discussions are confidential and without prejudice
  • Flexible Format: Can be conducted in person, via video conference, or through written submissions

Timeline: Mediation sessions typically scheduled within 10-15 business days of complaint assessment.

3Mediation Outcome

The mediator works with all parties to reach a mutually acceptable resolution:

  • Agreement Reached: If parties agree, a written settlement agreement is prepared
  • Partial Resolution: Some issues may be resolved while others proceed to next stage
  • No Agreement: If mediation doesn't resolve the dispute, parties can proceed to Disputes Tribunal

Success Rate: Over 75% of disputes are resolved through mediation, avoiding the need for tribunal proceedings.

4Disputes Tribunal Representation

If mediation doesn't resolve the dispute, NZIFDA can represent complainants at the Disputes Tribunal:

  • Free Representation: No legal fees for NZIFDA members and consumers who used certified operators
  • Expert Preparation: We prepare all documentation, evidence, and submissions
  • Technical Expertise: Our representatives understand fuel contamination remediation and can present technical evidence
  • Full Support: We handle the entire tribunal process on behalf of the complainant
  • No Win, No Fee: If the tribunal doesn't award in your favor, you pay nothing

Eligibility: Available for disputes involving certified operators, member insurers, or where the complaint relates to NZIFDA certification standards.

Types of Disputes We Handle

Consumer Disputes

  • Disputes about repair quality or completeness
  • Disputes about excessive charges or unnecessary work
  • Disputes about warranty coverage or follow-up issues
  • Disputes about operator conduct or service standards

Insurer Disputes

  • Disputes about claim costs or charges
  • Disputes about repair quality or compliance
  • Disputes about unnecessary parts or work (fuel pumps, injectors, etc.)
  • Disputes about operator compliance with insurance requirements

Fuel Station Disputes

  • Disputes about operator compliance or certification
  • Disputes about service quality or safety
  • Disputes about environmental compliance or waste tracking

Operator Disputes

  • Disputes about payment or invoicing
  • Disputes about certification requirements or compliance
  • Disputes about standards or procedures

Disputes Tribunal Process

What is the Disputes Tribunal?

The Disputes Tribunal is a New Zealand court that handles disputes up to $30,000. It's designed to be:

  • Accessible: No lawyers required, informal process
  • Fast: Hearings typically scheduled within 6-8 weeks
  • Low Cost: Filing fees are minimal ($45-$180 depending on claim amount)
  • Final: Tribunal decisions are binding and enforceable

NZIFDA Representation at Tribunal

When NZIFDA represents you at the Disputes Tribunal, we:

  • Prepare Your Case: Gather all evidence, documentation, and technical assessments
  • File the Claim: Complete all tribunal paperwork and filing requirements
  • Present Your Case: Represent you at the tribunal hearing
  • Handle Appeals: Manage any appeals or follow-up proceedings if necessary
  • Enforce Decisions: Assist with enforcement if the tribunal awards in your favor

Cost: Free for eligible parties. We cover all tribunal fees and costs.

How to Request Mediation

Start the Process

To request mediation or file a complaint:

  1. File a Complaint: Complete our complaint form with details of your dispute
  2. Provide Documentation: Include all relevant documents, invoices, correspondence, and evidence
  3. Initial Assessment: We'll review your complaint and contact you within 5 business days
  4. Mediation Arranged: If suitable, we'll arrange a mediation session within 10-15 business days

Eligibility & Costs

Who is Eligible for Free Mediation?

  • NZIFDA Member Insurers: Free mediation for all disputes involving member insurers
  • Policyholders of Member Insurers: Free mediation when using certified operators
  • Consumers Using Certified Operators: Free mediation for disputes with NZIFDA-certified operators
  • Fuel Stations: Free mediation for disputes involving certified operators

Costs for Non-Members

For disputes not involving certified operators or member insurers, mediation may be available at a reduced cost. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

Confidentiality & Privacy

All mediation discussions are:

  • Confidential: Information shared in mediation cannot be used in tribunal proceedings
  • Without Prejudice: Offers and discussions cannot be used as evidence
  • Private: Only parties involved and the mediator are present
  • Protected: All documentation is handled in accordance with privacy laws
