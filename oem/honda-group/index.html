⚠️ Honda Position

Honda prescribes fuel tank removal and/or fuel pump access as the procedures for accessing fuel tank contents.

Honda warranty excludes damage from fuel contamination as vehicle misuse. Honda Service Express (i-HDS) documents specific procedures that NZIFDA certified technicians follow.

Honda Service Manual Procedure

"Fuel Tank Removal

  • Relieve fuel pressure (disconnect fuel pump connector, start engine until stall)
  • Disconnect negative battery cable
  • Remove rear seat cushion and access panel
  • Disconnect fuel pump harness connector
  • Drain fuel using approved fuel recovery equipment
  • Raise vehicle and support safely
  • Disconnect fuel feed line and return line
  • Disconnect EVAP lines
  • Remove fuel tank protector
  • Support tank and remove mounting bolts
  • Lower tank"
— Honda Service Manual (i-HDS)

Honda Fuel System Notes

Honda vehicles in New Zealand are predominantly petrol-powered. Key considerations:

  • No diesel models - Honda does not sell diesel vehicles in NZ, reducing misfuel risk
  • Hybrid systems - e:HEV models still use petrol and can be contaminated
  • Direct injection - Newer models (1.5T, 2.0T) use direct injection, more sensitive to contamination

Diesel-in-Petrol Contamination

While less common, adding diesel to a Honda petrol engine requires:

  • Complete fuel system drain
  • Fuel filter replacement
  • System flush before refilling with petrol
  • Direct injection models may require injector inspection

✓ Honda Hybrid (e:HEV) Systems

Honda's e:HEV hybrid system uses a petrol engine as a generator with electric motor drive. The petrol tank can still be contaminated. If fuel contamination is suspected, do not start the vehicle as the engine will activate during system initialization.
