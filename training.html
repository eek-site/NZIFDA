Training Programs

NZIFDA offers comprehensive training programs to help operators meet and maintain certification standards. Our training ensures operators have the knowledge and skills to provide safe, compliant, and effective misfuel remediation services.

Available Training Courses

Fundamentals of Misfuel Remediation

Duration: 2 days | Level: Beginner

This comprehensive course covers the basics of misfuel remediation:

  • Understanding misfuels and contamination types
  • Fuel system components and operation
  • Extraction procedures and equipment
  • System flushing and remediation
  • Component replacement protocols
  • Basic documentation requirements

Who Should Attend: New operators, technicians entering the field

Advanced Remediation Techniques

Duration: 3 days | Level: Advanced

Advanced training for experienced operators:

  • Complex contamination scenarios
  • High-performance and specialty vehicles
  • Advanced diagnostic techniques
  • System optimization and testing
  • Troubleshooting and problem-solving
  • Quality assurance protocols

Prerequisites: Fundamentals course or equivalent experience

Regulatory Compliance Training

Duration: 1 day | Level: All Levels

Essential compliance training covering:

  • EPA Hazardous Substances Regulations
  • Dangerous Goods Act requirements
  • MfE waste disposal guidelines
  • Health and Safety at Work Regulations
  • Fuel Industry Act compliance
  • NZIFDA standards and requirements
  • Documentation and record-keeping

Who Should Attend: All operators and management staff

Waste Management and Disposal

Duration: 1 day | Level: Intermediate

Comprehensive waste management training:

  • Classification of contaminated fuel
  • Waste tracking documentation
  • Storage and handling requirements
  • Transportation regulations
  • Disposal facility requirements
  • Record retention and compliance

Equipment Operation and Maintenance

Duration: 1 day | Level: Intermediate

Training on certified equipment:

  • Vacuum extraction systems
  • Fuel system flushing equipment
  • Diagnostic tools and testing equipment
  • Maintenance and calibration
  • Safety procedures
  • Troubleshooting common issues

Customer Service and Documentation

Duration: Half day | Level: All Levels

Essential skills for customer interaction:

  • Professional communication
  • Transparent pricing and quotes
  • Warranty documentation
  • Dispute resolution
  • Customer education
  • Record-keeping best practices

Certification Requirements

To maintain NZIFDA certification, operators must:

  • Complete initial training requirements
  • Participate in annual refresher training
  • Stay current with regulatory updates
  • Maintain staff competency records

Training Schedule

Training courses are offered throughout the year at various locations across New Zealand. Custom on-site training is also available for larger organizations.
