⚠️ Isuzu Position

Isuzu prescribes fuel tank removal as the procedure for accessing fuel tank contents.

Isuzu diesel vehicles are particularly sensitive to fuel contamination. Warranty excludes damage from fuel contamination. NZIFDA certified technicians follow Isuzu service manual procedures.

About Isuzu Motors

Isuzu Motors Ltd. is a Japanese commercial vehicle and diesel engine manufacturer headquartered in Tokyo. Isuzu is one of the world's largest manufacturers of medium to heavy-duty trucks and is renowned for diesel engine expertise.

Isuzu Service Manual Procedure

"Fuel Tank Removal (D-Max)

WARNING: Fire hazard. Work in well-ventilated area away from sparks and flame.

  • Disconnect negative battery terminal
  • Remove fuel pump relay and start engine until stall to release pressure
  • Remove rear seat and access fuel pump connector
  • Drain fuel using approved fuel recovery equipment
  • Raise vehicle on hoist
  • Disconnect fuel filler hose and vent hose
  • Disconnect fuel feed and return lines
  • Support fuel tank with suitable jack
  • Remove fuel tank strap bolts
  • Lower fuel tank"
— Isuzu Service Manual

D-Max/MU-X Fuel Access

Current generation D-Max and MU-X have fuel pump access under rear seat, but tank drop is typically recommended for complete fuel contamination remediation.

⚠️ Isuzu Diesel - High Misfuel Risk Warning

Isuzu D-Max is one of the most commonly misfuelled vehicles in New Zealand. All D-Max and MU-X models are diesel-only and feature:

  • Common rail diesel injection - Extremely sensitive to petrol contamination
  • High-pressure fuel pump - Can fail catastrophically with wrong fuel
  • Piezoelectric injectors - Very expensive to replace if damaged

If Petrol Has Been Added:

  • Do NOT start the engine
  • Do NOT turn ignition to RUN
  • Contact NZIFDA certified technician immediately
  • Full tank drop and system flush required

Isuzu Commercial Trucks

Isuzu commercial trucks (N-Series, F-Series) require specialist knowledge for fuel contamination remediation:

  • Larger fuel tank capacities (up to 400L+)
  • Multiple tank configurations on some models
  • AdBlue/DEF systems requiring separate attention
  • Higher volumes of contaminated fuel to dispose of

NZIFDA certified technicians with commercial vehicle endorsement are recommended for truck fuel contamination.
