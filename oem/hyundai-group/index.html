⚠️ Hyundai Motor Group Position

Hyundai Motor Group prescribes fuel tank removal and/or fuel pump access panel removal as the procedures for accessing fuel tank contents.

Fuel contamination is classified as vehicle misuse and excluded from Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis warranty coverage. NZIFDA certified technicians follow manufacturer service manual procedures.

About Hyundai Motor Group

Hyundai Motor Group is South Korea's largest automotive manufacturer, headquartered in Seoul. The group operates three automotive brands covering different market segments.

Platform Sharing

Hyundai and Kia share platforms and many components, meaning fuel system procedures are often similar across brands:

  • Hyundai i30 / Kia Ceed share the K3 platform
  • Hyundai Tucson / Kia Sportage share the N3 platform
  • Hyundai Santa Fe / Kia Sorento share the N3 LWB platform

Hyundai/Kia Service Manual Procedure

"Fuel Tank Removal

1. Release residual pressure in fuel line

2. Disconnect negative (-) battery terminal

3. Remove fuel pump connector

4. Drain fuel from fuel tank using approved equipment

5. Raise vehicle and support safely

6. Disconnect fuel filler hose and vent hose

7. Disconnect fuel lines

8. Support tank with jack, remove mounting straps

9. Lower tank carefully"

— Hyundai/Kia GSW Service Manual

Fuel Pump Access

Many Hyundai and Kia models feature a fuel pump access panel under the rear seat, allowing pump removal without tank drop. Specific models include:

  • Hyundai i30, i40, Elantra
  • Hyundai Tucson, Santa Fe (most generations)
  • Kia Cerato, Sportage, Sorento

Electric Vehicles (Ioniq 5/6, EV6/EV9)

Hyundai Motor Group's dedicated electric vehicles do not have fuel tanks and therefore cannot be misfuelled. These include:

  • Hyundai Ioniq 5, Ioniq 6
  • Kia EV6, EV9
  • Genesis GV60

Note: Hybrid models (Tucson Hybrid, Sorento Hybrid, etc.) still have petrol tanks and can be subject to fuel contamination.
